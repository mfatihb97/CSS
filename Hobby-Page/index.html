Table Tennis

Table tennis, also known as ping-pong, is a fast-paced sport played by two or four players. Players use small paddles to hit a lightweight ball back and forth over a net on a table divided by a net. The aim is to score points by hitting the ball in a way that the opponent cannot return it properly. Table tennis requires quick reflexes, agility, and hand-eye coordination. The game can be played at various levels, from casual recreational play to highly competitive professional tournaments. It became an Olympic sport in 1988 and has since gained popularity worldwide due to its accessibility and thrilling gameplay.
